The Enterprise Architecture, Policy & Planning (IDRA) team provides strategic and policy support for GSA customers across the entire organization. Enterprise Architecture (EA) is responsible for providing the necessary support for designing organizational changes based on ever-evolving programmatic requirements. EA is involved in business transformation planning through the modeling of existing processes and environments and the development of roadmaps to reach the desired target states. In addition, EA is responsible for assessing the impact of change across the organization such as business, application and technological layers.

For more information about Enterprise Architecture and how EA can help GSA employees and organizations please see the presentation "Why EA?".


Sources of Data

The sources of the data are through surveys and data calls for linking business applications (FAS, PBS, CTO), business capabilities (EA), FISMA Systems (from ISP organization), and IT Standards (managed by the CTO office). Business Application Rationalization

The General Services Administration’s (GSA’s) Enterprise Architecture program has developed an innovative approach to Business Application Rationalization, a business-focused, data-driven methodology to analyze the IT application portfolio and help guide IT investment decisions. This methodology is used to assess whether a business application should be tolerated, invested, migrated, or eliminated (TIME) and provides a consistent approach to coordinate discussions between business and IT stakeholders to discuss the strategic direction for GSA’s IT investments. In concert with GSA’s enterprise governance boards, the EA team offers recommendations, which inform the determination to retire, consolidate, replace or modernize GSA’s business applications. You can view the current TIME report generated by the Business Application Rationalization process here.

TIME

Tolerate: There are three levels of tolerate.


  • T1 are considered in "keep the lights on" mode and only critical fixes are completed (i.e., resolve issues only if the business application cannot be used at all or without major workarounds or for significant security vulnerabilities). No DM&E. Only O&M.
  • T2 are tolerated with enhancements. Mostly O&M with some DM&E.
  • T3 are tolerated, are likely to be considered for migration in the next 1 to 3 years and a migration plan is needed. Mostly O&M with DM&E for migration planning.

Invest: Business Application is being developed/upgraded to provide functionality due to new business requirement or elimination of current business applications. Must have an approved EBC or 50% or more of budget is DM&E. Business Applications will have implementation plans detailing the planned enhancements.


Migrate: There are two levels of migrate. Migration planning and funding to complete migration should be approved so the migration can begin in the current fiscal year.


  • M1 are considered business applications whose technology/platform/hardware are being modernized; business applications satisfy drivers to move to a new platform or technology. Technology focused.
  • M2 are considered business applications whose capabilities are migrating to new solution; business application satisfy drivers including improved integration with other business applications. Technology/platform/hardware should be modernized as part of this migration. Business focused (could include technology changes).

Eliminate: Business Application is no longer needed. Elimination date is known. Data migration plan is known.
