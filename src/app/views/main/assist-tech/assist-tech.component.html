GEAR Assistive Technology Explanations

For users of Assistive Technology, this page is intended to give you an overview of GEAR (the GSA EA Analytics and Reporting tool) to provide a frame of reference for further navigation.

The main landing page consists of six major navigation links to the left ("IT Strategy," GSA Enterprise," "Business Systems," "Security," "Technologies," "Enterprise Architecture," and "Additional Information") that open up to list different sub-sections (such as "IT Strategic Framework" or "IT Investments" under the "Strategy" navigation link). These navigation links are available on every one of the GEAR pages on the left side as well as at the top of the page, named "GEAR Navigation". If your computer's display is small (like a laptop screen), then the "GEAR Navigation" will appear under the "More Links" dropdown.

There are also four links on the top navigation bar ("About," the aforementioned "GEAR Navigation," "Assistive Technology" (this page), and "Contact/Feedback"). Except for the "GEAR Navigation" option, these links take you to descriptive pages (like this page) that are formatted differently than the main sections linked on the left of the page.

The "Business Capabilities Model" and "Organization Chart" links under the "GSA Enterprise" navigation link display dynamic charts that are interactive with a network of connected nodes, showing the relationships between capabilities or organizations. Accompanying each dynamic chart is a table that contains the same information. For example, under the "GSA Enterprise" navigation link, there is a "Business Capability Model" that visually depicts the same information that is available in the "Business Capability List."

If you discover a component of the GEAR site that causes a problem with your assistive technology, please let us know at ea_planning@gsa.gov. We strive to make this site equally accessible to everyone.
